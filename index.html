The traditional cuisine of Japan

Japanese - washoku

It's one of the most popular and influential cuisines in the world, with a long and storied history that dates back centuries. Is is based on rice with miso soup and other dishes; there is an emphasis on seasonal ingredients. Encompasses the regional and traditional foods of Japan, which have developed through centuries of political, economic, and social changes.

Side dishes often consist of fish, pickled vegetables, and vegetables cooked in broth.

Apart from the purely restaurant experiences of kaiseki-ryori, Japanese cuisine tends to be simple, approachable and unsophisticated in a certain way. It is best represented by the cooking in the small shops or at home, where dishes tend to be relatively easy to prepare.

Katsudon - Japanese:

カツ丼

is a popular Japanese food, a bowl of rice topped with a deep-fried breaded pork cutlet, egg, vegetables, and condiments. The dish takes its name from the Japanese words tonkatsu (for pork cutlet) and donburi (for rice bowl dish).

Tofu steak - Japanese:

豆腐ステーキ

is a quite famous Japanese dish, but not as popular as previous one. Made from fried tofu with soy and oyster sauces, some spices and seasonal toppings (such as green onion, mushrooms, ect).

Mochi - Japanese:

もち, 餅

made by pounding steamed short-grain Japanese sticky rice, called mochigome, with a wooden mallet during a ceremony called mochitsuki. This aerates and pulverizes the rice, which is what gives mochi its beloved texture. Traditional stuffed with anko (sweetened red bean paste) made from azuki beans. We usually enjoy this popular Japanese sweet with green tea.It is smoother than ice cream, so it melts in your mouth. It's made from a red bean paste, but is also sweet, so it still tastes like a dessert.

Miso soup - Japanese:

味噌汁

is a traditional Japanese soup that starts with a dashi stock and is flavored with miso paste. Dashi is an umami-rich stock made from dried seaweed and dried fish. Miso paste, meanwhile, is a paste made from soybeans, salt, and koji rice. The brothy soup usually contains tofu and green onions.

Japanese curry - Japanese:

カレー

is the dish where usual ingredients added in include potatoes, carrots, onions and meat. The sauce itself is usually made from curry powder or roux, which consists of a blend of basic Indian spices.While curry is prevalent in multiple countries. Japanese curry is usually thicker in texture, sweeter, and less spicy than its Thai or Indian counterparts. That sweetness often comes from the addition of an apple and/or some honey.
